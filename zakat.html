Important Information

Who Must Pay Zakat?

Zakat is obligatory upon every Muslim who possesses wealth above the Nisab threshold for one lunar year. The Nisab is equivalent to 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver.

When is Zakat Due?

Zakat becomes due after your wealth exceeds the Nisab for a full lunar year (Hawl). Many Muslims pay during Ramadan, though it can be paid at any time once due.

What Wealth is Zakatable?

  • Gold and silver (jewelry, coins, bars)
  • Cash and bank balances
  • Business inventory and trade goods
  • Stocks, shares, and investments
  • Agricultural produce
  • Livestock
